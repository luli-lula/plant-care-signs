Rock Garden Thrives in rocky, well-drained spots

Did You Know?

Despite being a sedge typically associated with moisture, Ivory Sedge is remarkably heat and drought tolerant, thriving on dry sandy limestone outcroppings.
It spreads slowly from rhizomes and is never aggressive, making it an ideal well-mannered ground cover for shady rock gardens.
The species name 'eburnea' means ivory-white, referring to the pale color of its tiny fruits.

Planting Guide

  1. Plant plugs in part to full shade in well-drained soil.
  2. Space 8 inches apart in rock gardens or along paths.
  3. Drought-tolerant once established; no supplemental water needed.
